Pão de Queijo Recipe

The Brazilian pão de queijo is a staple from the state of Minas Gerais. Characterized by a crunchy exterior and a soft chewy interior it is the perfect accompaniment for any meal! Satisfy a sweet tooth or a savoury craving with this easy recipe.

Ingredients

Recipe

  1. Preset oven to 400 degrees Fahrenheit. Cut parchment paper to fit the cookie sheet.
  2. Simmer milk and oil: Combine the milk, oil, and salt in a saucepan. Gently bring to a simmer over medium heat whisking occasionally. As soon as big bubbles appear remove from heat.
  3. Combine with Tapioca starch: In a large mixing bowl add tapioca starch, slowly pour in milk and oil mixture, and then mix until incorporated fully. The dough will appear grainy and gelatinous.
  4. Mix in eggs: Crack eggs, whisk together until smooth, and then add to dough slowly mixing until incorporated fully.
  5. Add cheese: Add shredded Mozzarella and shredded Parmesan cheese to dough and mixing until incorporated fully. The resulting dough will be stretchy, sticky, and soft.
  6. Portion the dough: Roll 1'' balls and place on parchment paper on a cookie sheet. Space the dough an inch and a half apart.
  7. Finishing touches:Sprinkle a pinch of Parmesan cheese on top of each pão de queijo.
  8. Bake: Bake for 30 minutes, or until top turns golden brown and begin to crack.
  9. Serve warm, right from the oven!

Serving

Traditionally pão de queijo is served with butter. Try a sweet version serve with jam, jelly, or Nutella or for a savoury variation serve with cheese, avocado, and or sun dried tomato.

Cook's Notes

Tapioca starch gives this recipe the unique flavour and texture; there are no substitutes. It can be found in Latin markets or international grocery stores.

Leftover pão de queijo should be frozen the day of and then reheated in a toaster oven when desired.
